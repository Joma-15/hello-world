Method 1: Elimination Method

Description:

The elimination method involves manipulating the equations to eliminate one variable at a time, making it easier to solve the remaining equations.

Example:

Consider the following system of equations:

  • x + y + z = 6
  • 2x - y + 3z = 14
  • 3x + 4y - z = 3

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. Choose two equations to eliminate one variable.
  2. Use Equations 1 and 2 to eliminate z:
    Rearranging Equation 1: z = 6 - x - y

Method 2: Matrix Method (Gaussian Elimination)

Description:

This method involves transforming the system of equations into an augmented matrix and then using row operations to reach row-echelon form.

Example:

Consider the same system of equations:

  • x + y + z = 6
  • 2x - y + 3z = 14
  • 3x + 4y - z = 3

Conclusion

Both the elimination method and the matrix method (Gaussian elimination) are effective techniques for solving simultaneous equations with three variables. The choice of method often depends on the specific problem and personal preference.
